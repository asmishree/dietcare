It's no secret that maintaining a healthy BMI is crucial for your overall health. But what exactly is BMI, and how can you ensure you’re staying within the healthy range?

The BMI is essentially a measure of body fat based on your height and weight. It's calculated by dividing your weight by the square of your height. While it's not a perfect metric (sorry, math lovers), it does provide a rough estimate of body fat percentage. We’re not going to do the math here but see below for more info on how to get your BMI.

According to the US Preventive Services Task Force, by factoring in both your height and weight (and not just your weight alone), your BMI helps determine if you’re at risk for certain diseases.

So, what’s a healthy BMI?

Great question! Here’s the breakdown of the Adult Body Mass Index so you know where you’re at:


  • Anything below 18.5 is underweight
  • A BMI between 18.5 and 25 is considered healthy
  • A BMI between 25 and 30 is considered overweight
  • A BMI over 30 is considered obese


Before you start Googling the latest diet trend, keep in mind that BMI isn't a one-size-fits-all kind of thing. Athletes and people with more muscle mass may have a higher BMI — even if they’re actually fit and healthy. So, if you’re a gym buff (or something like that), it's always a good idea to talk to your doctor to make sure your body fat is in balance.

Why’s it so important to maintain a healthy BMI?

It’s important to pay attention to your body and what it's trying to tell you. Being overweight or obese can impact your quality of life and lead to a variety of health issues. This includes:

High blood pressure
High cholesterol levels
Diabetes
Heart disease
Stroke
Gallbladder disease
Osteoarthritis
Sleep apnea
Chronic inflammation and pain
Certain types of cancer
Mental disorders such as anxiety and depression
Increased risk of death


How do I measure my BMI anyway?


Your doctor usually does it for you during your annual physical by measuring your height and weight, and charting it accordingly. But if you want to stay on top of things between doctor visits, you can always weigh yourself and use a BMI calculator for up-to-date measurements.

How do I maintain a healthy BMI?

If your BMI isn't where it should be, don't worry – small changes can make a big difference. Staying active is a great place to start. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity per week. And don't forget to fuel your body with a balanced diet and, of course, stay hydrated.

Should I talk to a doctor about my BMI?

If your BMI score is outside of the healthy percentile, it’s important to talk to a provider. (You’ll thank yourself later.) Your primary care doctor is a great go-to for advice on ways to achieve a healthy balance of body fat. You could also see a dietician for further support.

Your BMI isn’t just a number, it’s a tool to help you live your healthiest life. Wherever you’re at, know that you’re on the right track just by reading this!
